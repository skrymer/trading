# Regime read-out v2 is accepted with limitations — a CRISIS detector with a precision-only THRUST; GRIND/NARROW/CHOP are descriptive-only and ungateable

The v1 pre-registration (ADR 0023) failed its first-compute anchor check (18/19 spans, 2026-06-12) and was revised under the pre-registration's own §5 loop — legally, since no strategy had been scored against it. The quant-adjudicated v2: the gap's equal-weight leg moved from the **mean to the median** per-name return (the mean is tail-contaminated by micro-cap moonshots/bad prints — gap p1 was −99% — and non-stationary across the platform's year-over-year data-quality drift; the clean median leg validated at 80.7% sign-agreement vs SPY−RSP), the gap cut became **asymmetric percentile terciles** frozen at the p33/p67 of the clean Block-A distribution (−0.007746 / +0.003167, read once by a pre-registered rule), a **second CRISIS leg** was added (close-basis drawdown ≤ −20% from the trailing 252-bar high — the correlated-decline signature of slow bears the velocity-sensing washout structurally misses; dwell-debounced entry, washout entry stays immediate), the **dispersion trust guard was demoted to an advisory thin-N flag** (fail-closed it blanked ~30% of every year, concentrated in crashes/recoveries — fail-blind), and **NARROW's direction leg weakened to not-DOWN** (un-starves melt-ups while refusing the bear-masquerade: mega-caps falling less than the median stock must not read as an up-tape regime). The sole anchor amendment: the 2022 CRISIS span corrected to the consensus bear (2022-06→10); a ground-truth fix made before the run, held regardless of outcome.

**Cycle-2 verdict (2026-06-13): 7/19 spans pass — accepted with limitations, params frozen, no further iteration.**

## Per-label trust grades and the consumer permission matrix

- **CRISIS — Grade A, trustworthy, fully gateable.** 5/7 spans (GFC 89%, 2011 83%, 2018-Q4 79%, 2002 73%, COVID 69%). Both misses are anchor-vs-definition artifacts: CRISIS asserts "in or recovering from a ≥20% drawdown / sustained washout" — it is a **confirmation, not an early warning**, and does not fire in a topping phase before −20% is reached (the 2000-09 span, recorded as a caveated FAIL on the frozen tally, not amended).
- **THRUST — Grade B, precision-only, gateable author-beware.** Where unmasked it is real (2003: 71%). **The drawdown-recovery blind spot (documented structural interaction):** the dd-leg's precedence means the entire post-crash recovery — roughly the 12 months SPY remains >20% below its trailing high — publishes CRISIS regardless of thrust (2009-Q2/Q3: 0%, 2020-Q2/Q3: 27%). An accepted trade-off for catching slow bears, not a defect to tune out. Deploy-in-uptrend intent should use the leadership-gap regime (ADR 0010), not THRUST.
- **GRIND / NARROW / CHOP — Grade D, descriptive-only, gating FORBIDDEN.** Three cheap daily axes cannot separate grind-from-chop nor narrow-from-chop (coverage 0–48%); CHOP is a residual grab-bag (49% of days, only 36% direction-flat), asserting "unclassified", not "choppy". `RegimeLabelCondition`/`RegimeLabelExitCondition` reject these labels at build time with an explicit error — a noise gate would be locked in by the rescue-forbidden boundary (ADR 0023) with no legal repair path. The assessment's regime table shows their rows only under a fixed reliability banner; the current-regime line collapses them into one "uptrend — fine-grain label unreliable" state.

Stability is a near-miss (median spell 13d vs 15 floor; 13.0 flips/yr vs 12) and lives inside the GRIND/NARROW/CHOP band — the stream is consumed as CRISIS-vs-not plus a precision-only THRUST, never as a clean five-state series.

## No v3 by iteration

Shifting bands, retuning the dwell, re-ordering the dd/THRUST precedence, or adding sub-filters to chase the failing labels is IS-fitting to anchors now seen — forbidden, permanently. A legitimate v3 is a **new pre-registration from scratch**: a structurally new axis (e.g. cross-sectional dispersion/correlation, sector-participation, vol term-structure), parameters derived by a rule fixed before any coverage is computed, validated against **uncontaminated ground truth** (held-out spans or an externally-defined series — the 19 anchor spans are burned as a primary gate), under the same unrelaxed acceptance protocol.

## Amendment 2026-06-14 — the "cross-sectional dispersion" example is superseded for NARROW

The v2 decision above is unchanged. This clarifies one illustrative item in *"No v3 by iteration"*: the example list of structurally-new axes names *"cross-sectional dispersion/correlation"*. A `/grill-with-docs` session with three independent quant reads (issue #168) found **cross-sectional return dispersion is the wrong axis for NARROW** and should not be read as a sanctioned v3 path:

- Top−bottom (and full-universe) cross-sectional **dispersion is cap-blind** — a small-cap surge and a mega-cap surge produce identical dispersion but opposite regimes; cap-identity *is* the NARROW signal. It is also ~1.60σ-collinear with the full-universe stdev that CONTEXT.md ("Market regimes") already **ruled out** (2026-06-03), and was already shown *fail-blind* on our own data (v1's dispersion guard fired symmetrically in crashes and recoveries — demoted to the advisory thin-N flag).
- The genuine missing channel is **multi-week trend-efficiency** (Choppiness Index / Kaufman ER on SPY, frozen window) for **GRIND-vs-CHOP**; **NARROW** requires a **cap-weighted** concentration construct over a point-in-time **top-N-by-cap** universe (the count-equal STOCK universe is structurally blind to mega-cap concentration), which needs a new market-cap primitive (issues #173 / #174).

So the v3 NARROW axis is **cap-weighted return-concentration**, not cross-sectional dispersion. *"Correlation"* (cross-name co-movement) and *"sector-participation"* remain legitimate illustrative examples; bare *"dispersion"* does not. Everything else in this ADR — v2 frozen, no-v3-by-iteration, the from-scratch + uncontaminated-ground-truth protocol — stands.
